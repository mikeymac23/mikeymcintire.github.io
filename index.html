How I Became a Dawg

Ever since I was a little kid I wanted to go to The University of Georgia. Although it may have seemed it took a while for me to get here, I am here now. I went to a little school about 45 minutes away from Georgia's campus called Lincoln County High School. We are known for football and also one of Georgia's greats Garrison Hearsts. I forgot to mention my dream was, well still is to play for UGA. I went to go play football at Kennesaw State for two years and now I am here at UGA to pursue my dreams. If I am unable to play for UGA, I would like to go another route and becaome a Grad Assistant for the coaching staff. Walking out of that tunnel on to field in Sanford Stadium would just a dream come true.To see the whole stadium packed and cheering just gives me goosebumps every time thinking about it. Finally if all else fails though, I am still going to the best school in the world. No school compares to the atmosphere that this little town of Athens has. Some call it the Classic City, some call it the greatest college town in America. I'm just glad to call it home.

UGA Football

Here at UGA football means a lot to everyone and over the past couple of years we have been dealt with some very bad losses. Overall nothing can beat a Saturday in between the hedges in good ol' Athens, Georgia. We bleed red and black and no matter what we will be screaming "Go Dawgs".

The Blackout

Georgia brought out the black jerseys for the first time in 2007 when we faced our rival Auburn. That game goes down as one of my favorites, because the atmosphere that night was surreal and everything was clicking for the Dawgs.We went on to win that night 45-20 By far one of my best memories as a football fan. I will get to create more memories on Saturday, April 22, 2017 at 2:00 p.m. which will be Georgia's Spring game and my first game attending as a student here.
